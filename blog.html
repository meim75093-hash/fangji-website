5 Natural Ways to Manage Gout

Gout is a disease caused by excessive uric acid levels in the body, leading to uric acid crystal deposition in joints. Besides medication, natural therapies can effectively help control gout flare-ups.">Gout is a disease caused by excessive uric acid levels in the body, leading to uric acid crystal deposition in joints. Besides medication, natural therapies can effectively help control gout flare-ups.

1. Dietary Adjustments

  • Reduce high-purine foods: organ meats, seafood, rich meat broths
  • Eat more alkaline foods: vegetables, fruits, milk to help neutralize uric acid
  • Limit alcohol intake: especially beer, which inhibits uric acid excretion

2. Stay Hydrated

Daily water intake should reach 2000-3000ml to promote uric acid excretion through urine. You can drink plain water, light tea, or water with lemon slices.

3. Herbal Support

Fangji Root has the effects of dispelling wind and dampness, promoting diuresis and reducing swelling, which can help promote uric acid metabolism. You can drink Fangji wellness tea daily, or use Fangji Root formulas under medical guidance.

4. Moderate Exercise

Regular low-intensity exercise such as walking, swimming, Tai Chi can promote blood circulation and help uric acid metabolism. However, rest during acute flare-ups and avoid strenuous exercise.

5. Weight Management

Obesity increases gout risk, and weight loss can reduce uric acid levels. However, avoid rapid weight loss as it may cause temporary uric acid elevation.

Wellness Tips:

The above methods are for reference only. Gout patients should receive standardized treatment under medical guidance. Fangji Root products are wellness products and cannot replace medical treatment.

Understanding Fangji Root

Fangji Root (scientific name: Stephania tetrandra S. Moore), also known as Han Fangji or Shi Chan Chu, is the dried root of the Menispermaceae plant Stephania tetrandra. As a traditional Chinese medicinal herb, Fangji Root has over 2000 years of medicinal history.">Fangji Root (scientific name: Stephania tetrandra S. Moore), also known as Han Fangji or Shi Chan Chu, is the dried root of the Menispermaceae plant Stephania tetrandra. As a traditional Chinese medicinal herb, Fangji Root has over 2000 years of medicinal history.

Historical Records

Fangji Root was first recorded in 'Shennong Ben Cao Jing' and classified as a lower-grade herb. It has been documented in鍘嗕唬 herbal texts and is a commonly used herb in TCM for dispelling wind-dampness and relieving pain. Mainly produced in Zhejiang, Anhui, Jiangxi, and Fujian, with Zhejiang producing the best quality.

Properties & Meridians

Properties: Bitter, acrid, cold

Meridians: Bladder, Lung

Functions: Dispels wind and relieves pain, promotes diuresis and reduces swelling

Indications: Rheumatic pain, joint swelling, edema, difficult urination

Active Compounds

Modern pharmacological research shows that Fangji Root mainly contains the following alkaloid compounds:

  • Tetrandrine: Main active compound with significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and immunomodulatory effects">Tetrandrine: Main active compound with significant anti-inflammatory, analgesic, and immunomodulatory effects
  • Fangchinoline: Secondary active compound with antioxidant and anti-tumor effects
  • Cyclanoline: Has blood pressure-lowering and sedative effects">Cyclanoline: Has blood pressure-lowering and sedative effects

Modern Applications

Modern medical research has found that tetrandrine has multiple pharmacological effects and has been widely used in:

  • Treatment of rheumatic arthritis and rheumatoid arthritis
  • Adjunctive treatment for hypertension
  • Prevention and treatment of silicosis
  • Adjunctive therapy for cancer radiotherapy and chemotherapy
Usage Precautions:

Fangji Root is cold in nature; use with caution for those with weak spleen and stomach. Pregnant women, nursing mothers, and children should use under medical guidance. This is a wellness product and cannot replace medical treatment.

Traditional Chinese Medicine for Rheumatism

Rheumatism in TCM belongs to the category of 'Bi Syndrome' (鐥硅瘉), caused by external pathogens such as wind, cold, dampness, and heat invading the body, leading to meridian blockage and impaired qi and blood flow, primarily manifesting as limb joint pain, soreness, numbness, heaviness, and movement difficulties.

TCM Syndrome Differentiation

1. Wandering Bi (Wind Predominance)

Symptoms: Migratory joint pain, aversion to wind and fever

Treatment: Dispel wind, unblock collaterals, scatter cold and dampness

2. Painful Bi (Cold Predominance)

Symptoms: Severe joint pain, worsened by cold, relieved by warmth

Treatment: Warm meridians, scatter cold, dispel wind and dampness

3. Fixed Bi (Dampness Predominance)

Symptoms: Heavy and sore joints, fixed position, worsened on rainy days

Treatment: Dispel dampness, unblock collaterals, dispel wind and scatter cold

4. Heat Bi (Heat Predominance)

Symptoms: Red, swollen, hot and painful joints, fever and thirst

Treatment: Clear heat, unblock collaterals, dispel wind and dampness

Commonly Used Herbs

  • Fangji Root: Dispels wind and relieves pain, promotes diuresis and reduces swelling, suitable for rheumatic pain and joint swelling
  • Du Huo: Dispels wind and dampness, unblocks bi and relieves pain, suitable for wind-cold-damp bi">Du Huo: Dispels wind and dampness, unblocks bi and relieves pain, suitable for wind-cold-damp bi
  • Qin Jiao: Dispels wind-dampness, clears damp-heat, relieves bi pain">Qin Jiao: Dispels wind-dampness, clears damp-heat, relieves bi pain
  • Wei Ling Xian: Dispels wind-dampness, unblocks collaterals, suitable for rheumatic pain">Wei Ling Xian: Dispels wind-dampness, unblocks collaterals, suitable for rheumatic pain

Daily Wellness Recommendations

  • Keep warm and dry: Protect joints from cold and dampness, reduce outdoor activities on rainy days">Keep warm and dry: Protect joints from cold and dampness, reduce outdoor activities on rainy days
  • Moderate exercise: Choose low-intensity exercises like Tai Chi, Ba Duan Jin, walking to maintain joint mobility">Moderate exercise: Choose low-intensity exercises like Tai Chi, Ba Duan Jin, walking to maintain joint mobility
  • Dietary care: Avoid cold and greasy foods, eat more warming foods like ginger, scallion, lamb
  • Herbal support: Use wind-dampness dispelling herbs like Fangji Root under medical guidance, drink wellness tea daily
Wellness Tips:

Rheumatism is a chronic condition requiring persistent care. TCM treatment emphasizes syndrome differentiation; standardized treatment under TCM practitioner guidance is recommended. Fangji Root products are wellness products and cannot replace medical treatment.
