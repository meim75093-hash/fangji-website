About Fangji Root

Used for Thousands of Years

📜 Historical Origins

Fangji Root, scientifically named Stephania tetrandra S. Moore, is the dried root of the Menispermaceae plant. First recorded in 'Shennong Ben Cao Jing', it has been documented in历代 herbal texts and is a commonly used herb in TCM for dispelling wind-dampness and relieving pain. Mainly produced in Zhejiang, Anhui, and Jiangxi, with Zhejiang producing the best quality.

🌿 Properties & Meridians

Properties: Bitter, acrid, cold
Meridians: Bladder, Lung
Functions: Dispels wind and relieves pain, promotes diuresis and reduces swelling
Indications: Rheumatic pain, joint swelling, edema, difficult urination

🔬 Modern Research

Modern pharmacological research shows that Fangji Root mainly contains alkaloids such as tetrandrine and fangchinoline, with anti-inflammatory, analgesic, immunomodulatory, and anti-tumor effects. Tetrandrine is its main active ingredient and has been widely used in clinical treatment.

⚠️ Usage Precautions

1. This product is bitter and cold, use with caution for those with weak spleen and stomach
2. Pregnant women, nursing mothers, and children should use under medical guidance
3. Not suitable for long-term large-dose consumption
4. If taking other medications, consult a physician or pharmacist before use
5. This is a wellness product and cannot replace medical treatment

🏞️ Origin & Regions

Core Regions: Our Fangji Root is primarily sourced from authentic producing areas in Jinhua, Zhejiang and Huangshan, Anhui. These regions have a mild climate, abundant rainfall, and fertile soil, ideal for Fangji Root cultivation.

Growing Environment: Fangji Root prefers sunny conditions, is cold and drought resistant, and typically grows in shrubs at altitudes of 420-1,300 meters. Our partner cultivation bases are located in pollution-free natural environments to ensure the natural quality of the herbs.

Harvest & Processing: Fangji Root is typically harvested in autumn after the stems and leaves wither, when the active compound content in the roots is highest. After harvesting, it undergoes traditional processing including cleaning, slicing, and drying to preserve the best quality.
